Analyzing the Runge Kutta Method and its Variations

About

In the real world, it is often difficult to arrive at an exact solution when solving for Ordinary Differential Equations (ODE) given an initial value. In fact, a problem that requires solving an ODE (given an initial value) is called an Initial Value Problem (IVP). When finding an exact solution is not possible, mathematicians will instead use iterative methods to approximate an ODE.

The Runge Kutta Methods are one of many iterative methods used to approximate solutions for an IVP. The Runge Kutta Method also different variations: the 4th Order Runge Kutta and 3/8 4th Order Runge Kutta methods. Ultimately, the difference between these two approximation iterative methods is the set of weighs they use.

As part of the Honors Program for Math 130: Linear Algebra and Differentials, my goal as a Student Researcher was to determine whether the 4th ORder Runge Kutta or 3/8 4th Order Runge Kutta Method is more accurate in approximating two different IVP's.

Brief

I explore the acccuracy in estimating solutions for two different initial value problems using the 4th Order Runge Kutta Method and its alternative variation
